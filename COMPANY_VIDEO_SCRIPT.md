## POINT 3: THE TEAM

I have 12 team members. None of them are human. All of them work 24 hours a day. And they're getting smarter every week.
*Whiteboard — clean section. Write ".claude/agents/" at the top. Draw four folders: "platform/", "growth/", "retention/", "support/".*

Let me introduce them.
*Point to "platform/" folder.*

Platform engineering. Five agents. Marcus is my build engineer. Every time code changes, he runs the build, catches type errors, and fixes them before they hit production. Amara is systems reliability — she audits every scheduled job, every day, and flags anything that silently stopped working. Kai handles deployments. Priya validates database migrations. Devon runs security — dependency audits, vulnerability scans, supply chain checks.
*Whiteboard — under "platform/", write each name as you say it: Marcus, Amara, Kai, Priya, Devon. Next to each, a one-word role: "build", "reliability", "deploy", "database", "security".*

Growth. Three agents. Zara is my growth strategist. Every Monday she analyzes the full acquisition funnel and finds the one bottleneck that matters. Miles tracks recruiter ROI — every dollar I spend on artist acquisition, he tells me if it's working. Luna manages artist onboarding — she's obsessed with getting every new artist to their first subscriber as fast as possible.
*Under "growth/": Zara, Miles, Luna. Roles: "funnel", "partners", "onboarding".*

Retention. Three agents. Reese analyzes churn patterns across every artist on the platform — things no individual artist agent can see. Nadia runs pricing analysis — she knows exactly what price point maximizes lifetime value, not just conversions. And Orion. Orion is the intelligence director. He takes every outcome from every agent across every artist and builds a shared playbook. When one artist's re-engagement campaign works, every other artist benefits from that data automatically.
*Under "retention/": Reese, Nadia, Orion. Roles: "churn", "pricing", "intelligence".*

And support. One agent. Sage is the customer success lead. An artist has a question — Sage looks up their account, diagnoses the issue, and drafts a response. Billing problem? Sage checks their Stripe status and tells me exactly what happened. A fan can't access content? Sage traces it to the tier permissions and writes the fix. First touch resolution, every time. And if something's too complex, Sage escalates it to me with full context so I never have to ask a follow-up question.
*Under "support/": Sage. Role: "customer success". Drawing shows a robot with a headset, an arrow from "artist question" to Sage to "drafted response". A second arrow from Sage to "ESCALATE → Joshua" for complex issues.*

*Pull back to show the full whiteboard: all 12 names organized under 4 departments. Hold for 3 seconds.*

That's the roster. But a list of names doesn't mean anything if the system doesn't actually work. Let me show you.
*Hold on whiteboard, then cut.*

Three of these agents run on a schedule in the cloud. Amara runs every morning. Devon runs every Monday. Zara runs every Monday. They check in, do their job, and report back. I don't touch anything.
*Screen recording: Claude Code scheduled agents dashboard showing the 3 triggers with their schedules and next run times.*

But the real power is what happens inside the platform every day. At 1 PM, the AI manager runs for every artist on the platform. It collects their data — revenue, subscribers, churn, plays, community engagement. It generates insights. And then it acts. Low-risk actions get executed automatically. High-risk actions get escalated for the artist to approve.
*Screen recording: AI Manager tab showing pending actions with Approve/Reject buttons, recent agent activity with green checkmarks.*

When an action executes, the system takes a snapshot of the artist's metrics. Seven days later, it measures again. Did MRR go up? Did churn go down? Did subscribers increase? That delta gets stored. And the next time the AI runs, it sees what worked and what didn't. It learns.
*Whiteboard — draw a simple loop: "Action" -> "Snapshot" -> "7 days" -> "Measure" -> "Learn" -> back to "Action". Write "FEEDBACK LOOP" above it.*

And then Orion takes those results from every artist and feeds them back to every other artist. One artist's win becomes every artist's advantage. The more artists on the platform, the smarter every agent gets.
*Tap the loop on the whiteboard. Circle "FEEDBACK LOOP" for emphasis.*

Every artist who joins brings fans. Every fan generates data. Every data point makes the agents smarter. Every agent improvement helps every artist. It's a flywheel. And it's already spinning.
*Whiteboard — draw a circular arrow connecting: "Artists" -> "Fans" -> "Data" -> "Smarter Agents" -> "Better Results" -> "More Artists". Label it "FLYWHEEL".*

This is why this company is different from every other pitch you'll hear today. Most startups hire people to solve problems. People cost money. People quit. People need managing. I built a team that costs almost nothing to run, works around the clock, and compounds in intelligence every single day. And every day we run, the moat gets deeper.
*Hold on the flywheel drawing for 3 seconds.*
