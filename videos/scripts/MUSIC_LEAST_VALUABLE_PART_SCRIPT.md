## POINT 1: HOW STREAMING TRAINED YOUR FANS TO EXPECT YOUR MUSIC FOR FREE

Think about the last time you paid for a song. Not a subscription. A song. One song. You gave someone money for it.

Most people can't remember. Maybe it was iTunes 10 years ago. Maybe it was never.

That's the problem. Music used to be something you bought. A CD was $15. An album on iTunes was $9.99. A single was $1.29. You paid for it because that was the only way to hear it.

Then streaming happened. And the price of a song went from $1.29 to $0.003. That's not a price drop. That's extinction.

Your fan used to pay $1.29 to hear your song once. Now they pay nothing and hear it forever.

And you can't blame them. You trained them. Every artist trained them. The entire industry trained them. You put your music on a platform that charges $10.99 for all the music in the world. Your song is one of 100 million. Why would anyone pay for yours specifically?

They wouldn't. And they don't.

And later I'm going to show you what the artists making real money are actually selling. It's not what you think. And once you see it, you can't unsee it.

So streaming trained your fans to expect your music for free. That's where we are. But some artists are still making thousands a month. What are they selling?

## POINT 2: WHY ARTISTS WILL DISAPPEAR

Every year, thousands of artists quit. Not because they ran out of talent. Because they ran out of money.

They made great music. They got streams. They posted every day. And they still couldn't pay rent from it. So they stopped.

The artists who disappear aren't the ones without talent. They're the ones who kept selling the one thing the market priced at zero. The song.

They thought if the music was good enough, the money would follow. It didn't. Because the music isn't what people pay for anymore. The market changed. They didn't.

And it's not just about making less money. It's about becoming invisible. When you only offer a song, and every other artist only offers a song, there is nothing to separate you. You're interchangeable. And interchangeable artists get replaced.

The artists who survive aren't the most talented. They're the ones who figured out what their real product is. But what is it?

And that brings us to the part I've been building toward this entire video. What the artists making real money are actually selling. When you see what that real product is, it's going to change how you think about your entire career.
