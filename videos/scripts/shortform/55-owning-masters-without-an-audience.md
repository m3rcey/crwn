Everybody yelling own your masters. Nobody telling you a master with no audience pays you nothing. Watch why.

Yall think the goal is just to own it. Owning it is half. The other half decides if it's worth a dollar.

A master is the recording. If you own it, every play, every sync, every sale comes back to you instead of a label. That's real. But a recording only makes money when people actually want it. Own 100% of a song nobody's looking for and you own 100% of zero.

The artists you watched get rich off ownership had two things, not one. They owned the music, and they had a crowd that already cared. The crowd is what turned the ownership into a check.

So flip the order everybody pushes. Build the people who'd pay you first. Then owning your music actually means something, because there's somebody on the other side of it.

Ownership is a safe. The audience is what you put in it. An empty safe with the best lock in the world is still empty.
