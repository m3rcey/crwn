**SCRIPT:**

Tech N9ne does over 150 shows a year. Every dollar from that tour flows into a label he owns. Most artists' tour money flows the other way. Watch the difference.

Yall grind a tour to pay the label back. He grinds a tour to fund the thing that's his.

Strange Music made the road a main revenue source on purpose. 150-plus nights a year. The merch table at the show is Strange Music merch. The label putting on the tour is the label he co-owns. So the ticket, the hoodie, the album in the booth, all of it lands in the same house, and the house is his.

Now the normal version. A signed artist tours. The label can take from the tour, take from the merch, recoup the marketing, all of it. The artist runs himself into the ground filling a vault somebody else holds the key to.

Tech runs himself into the ground too. The difference is where he's running. Toward something with his name on the deed, not away from a debt. The work is the same brutal grind. Who it builds is the whole game.
