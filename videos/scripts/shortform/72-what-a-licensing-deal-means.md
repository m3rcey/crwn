## WHAT A LICENSING DEAL IS, USING BURNA BOY

**CAPTION:**

You Can Lend A Major Your Music Without Selling It

Link in bio to keep ownership and just lend out the reach with CRWN. Free to start at thecrwn.app.

---

**SCRIPT:**

There's a deal where a major moves your music worldwide and you still own all of it. It's called a licensing deal. Burna Boy's setup shows you the shape.

Yall only know one kind of deal, the kind where they own you. There's a kind where you just lend it.

A record deal: the label owns the masters. They keep the bigger share. You recoup before you see real money. They have it. Maybe forever.

A licensing deal flips the part that matters. You keep the masters. The major doesn't buy your music, they borrow it for a set time to push it everywhere. They take a cut for that reach. When the time's up, the rights come all the way back to you. Burna Boy's albums run through his own Spaceship in partnership with a major. His house, their pipes.

Same big company on the paper. One version they own it. The other version they're renting it from you.

Selling means it's gone. Licensing means it's a loan you called back. Don't ask only what they'll pay. Ask do they own it or are they borrowing it.
