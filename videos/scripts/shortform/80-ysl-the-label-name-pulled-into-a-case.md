## YSL WAS A LABEL. PROSECUTORS SAID IT WAS A GANG.

**CAPTION:**

**SCRIPT:**

YSL is a record label. In court, prosecutors said those same three letters meant a gang. Same name, two readings, and the artist didn't get to pick which one stuck. Watch.

Yall think your label name is just branding. Brand a thing and somebody else can redefine it on you.

To the music world, YSL is Young Stoner Life. A record label. Gunna came out of it. Lil Baby came up through that world. Real artists, real careers under that name. But in the Georgia indictment, prosecutors argued YSL stood for Young Slime Life, and that it was a criminal street gang. One name. The label's identity got pulled straight into a criminal case.

Then it went further. Song lyrics got presented as evidence. The art itself, used against the artist. It got so heavy it sparked national debate and new laws about using rap lyrics in court.

Here's the lesson under the headline. When you don't control the story around your name and your catalog, somebody else writes it. The same three letters can be your brand to fans and a charge in a courtroom. Owning the work is also owning what it gets to mean.
