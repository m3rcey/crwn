## WHAT "SELF-RELEASE WITH A DISTRIBUTOR" LOOKS LIKE

**CAPTION:**

The Setup The Biggest Artists Quietly Use

Link in bio to self-release and still hit every store with CRWN. Free to start at thecrwn.app.

---

**SCRIPT:**

The biggest artists out aren't signed the way you think. A lot of them self-release and just hire a distributor. Here's exactly what that looks like.

Yall picture self-release as uploading alone in your room. The pro version is a whole different machine.

Two pieces. Piece one, your own company. It owns the music, picks the songs, runs the rollout, controls the look, keeps the bigger share. Piece two, a distributor. You pay them a cut to do one job, push your release into every store, every app, every country. They don't own it. They move it.

That's it. You own and decide. They deliver. The reach you thought required a label is just the delivery half, and you can rent that half by itself.

Beyonce's recent albums run through her own company with a major handling distribution. Mac Miller hit number 1 the same shape, his label plus a distributor. Same machine, different size.

Self-release with a distributor means you keep the wheel and pay somebody for the pipes. Owning it and reaching everybody were never a package deal. You can have both.
