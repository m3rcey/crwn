## WHEN YOUR LYRICS BECOME THE EVIDENCE

**CAPTION:**

They Put The Songs On Trial, Not Just The Artist

Link in bio to own the work before anyone else gets to use it with CRWN. Free to start at thecrwn.app.

**SCRIPT:**

In the YSL case, the rap lyrics themselves got entered as evidence. Not just what someone did. What they wrote in a song. Let me explain what that even means.

Yall write bars and think it's just art. In a courtroom, somebody can argue it's a confession.

Quick breakdown. RICO is a law built to treat a whole organization like a racket, so the name, the group, the associations all get pulled in together. In the Georgia case, prosecutors used song lyrics as part of the evidence to argue intent and membership. The verses an artist recorded years earlier got read back in court as proof.

That's heavy on its own. It got so loud it pushed real legislative debate, states moving to limit using rap lyrics as evidence at all.

Here's the lesson for you, not just the headline. Your catalog is not only money. It's a record of your words that outlives the moment and can be used by people you never met, for purposes you never intended. Who controls that catalog, and the story around it, matters before any of that ever happens.

The art is forever. So is who gets to point at it. Own it, and own what it's allowed to mean.
