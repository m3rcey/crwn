**SCRIPT:**

When an artist dies, the music keeps earning. The label debt keeps living too. That's recoupment after death. Let me show you how it works.

Yall think the contract ends when the artist does. The paper don't care that you're gone.

Recoupment is simple. The label fronts you money. An advance. Videos. Marketing. All of it gets charged back against your account before you see a real check. While you alive, you working it off. Here's the part nobody says out loud: that account doesn't close when you do. The estate inherits it. The balance the label says you still owe just transfers to your family along with the catalog they don't even own.

So the music keeps streaming. The checks keep coming. But they run through the label first, against a debt that followed the artist into the ground, paid back by people who never signed anything.

Think about DMX. The catalog still earns every day. It earns for whoever holds the masters and the account, not automatically for the kids.

A debt you can die still owing is not a deal. It's a mortgage on your own name. The only music with no debt attached is the music you owned from the start.
