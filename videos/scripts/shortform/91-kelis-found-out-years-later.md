**SCRIPT:**

Kelis says she made no money off her first two albums. She found out the real deal years after she signed it. Watch where it went wrong.

Yall trust the verbal agreement. The verbal agreement is the trap.

In her own words, in a 2020 interview, Kelis says she signed her first deal at 19. The understanding was simple, split everything three ways between her and the producers. Equal. She believed it. But it never got put in writing the way she thought. Years later, when she went looking for her money from those first two albums, she says there wasn't any for her. When she confronted them, she says the answer was basically, well, you signed it.

Sit with that. Not a small artist. A real one, hit records, and by her account, nothing from the first two, and she didn't fully see it until way later.

That's the lesson under the story. A split you can't see is not a split. A handshake at 19 is not a contract you understood. If you can't watch the money and read the terms, you don't actually know what you agreed to until it's too late to change it.

See it in writing. See it in real time. Or you're just hoping.
