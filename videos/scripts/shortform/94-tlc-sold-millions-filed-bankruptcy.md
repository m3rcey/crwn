**CAPTION:**

**SCRIPT:**

TLC sold tens of millions of records. Right after the biggest album of their career, they filed for bankruptcy. The sales were never the problem. Watch what was.

Yall think a Diamond album means the artist got paid. TLC is the proof it doesn't.

CrazySexyCool went Diamond. One of the best-selling albums by a group, period. Then in 1995 the three of them filed for bankruptcy. Not flopping. Not unknown. Diamond, and broke at the same time.

Here's where it went. They've said the deal left them somewhere around fifty something cents on an album, and that little piece got split three ways. Then management took its share off the top, and every video, every advance, every expense got charged back to them first. So you sell ten million, the math runs through everybody else, and what reaches the three of you is closer to a regular year's pay than a fortune.

That's the whole lesson. The records moving was never the question. The question was what reached them per record after the chain took its cut. Diamond plays don't matter if the split hands you pennies on each one.

A small cut on a huge number is still a small number. The artists who keep their money are the ones who kept the dollar in the room before anyone else got to it.
