**SCRIPT:**

A 19-year-old from Pittsburgh debuted at number 1 in the country with no major label. Hadn't happened in 16 years. Watch how.

Yall think a number 1 debut needs a major machine behind it. A teenager proved it doesn't.

November 2011. Mac Miller drops Blue Slide Park. His first album. He's 19. The label is Rostrum, an independent, distributed through an indie pipe, not a major. Opening week, 144,000 copies. Number 1 on the Billboard 200. First independently-distributed debut album to hit number 1 since 1995. Sixteen years nobody did it. A kid did it independent.

No major picked him out of a crowd. No major bankrolled it. He built the audience first, off mixtapes and a real fanbase, then dropped the album into people already there.

The number 1 spot was never gated behind a major. It was gated behind having an audience that actually shows up. He had that before he had a deal, so he didn't need the deal to win.

You don't need to be picked. You need people who already pull up. Build them first, and the chart stops being a major's to hand out.
