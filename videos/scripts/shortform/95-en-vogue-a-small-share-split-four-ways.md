## EN VOGUE SPLIT A SMALL ARTIST SHARE FOUR WAYS

**CAPTION:**

Four People, One Small Slice, Then The Math Got Worse

Link in bio to keep the whole dollar in the room with CRWN. Free to start at thecrwn.app.

---

**SCRIPT:**

En Vogue was one of the biggest groups of their time. Four members. Watch what four ways does to a slice that was already small.

Yall focus on how big the group was. The size of the group is the part that quietly works against them.

Start with how a group deal runs. The label keeps the majority. What's left over is the artist share, and that one slice is already the smaller piece before anybody in the group touches it. Now split that slice four ways. Then take out management, lawyers, and every expense the deal lets them charge back. Four people are now living off a quarter of the small piece, minus everything.

And when the structure stops working, it gets worse. By the EV3 era there was a real contract impasse, Dawn Robinson walked, and the group that sold all those records was arguing over a pie that was never that big to begin with.

That's the lesson. It was never about how many records four women moved. It was that the only piece they shared was the small one, and four ways turned small into almost nothing.

The math gets ugly when the dollar is divided after the label, not before. Keep the whole dollar in the room first, then split a real number instead of a leftover.
