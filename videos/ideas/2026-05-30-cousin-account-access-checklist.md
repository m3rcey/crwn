COUSIN ACCOUNT ACCESS - SECURITY CHECKLIST

Goal: let your cousin schedule posts without ever being able to take over your
account. Do these in order. Lock the takeover doors first, grant access second,
revoke last. Mark each with an x as you go.


PHASE 1 - LOCK THE TAKEOVER DOORS (do this BEFORE giving any access)

  [ ] Turn on two-factor authentication on your CRWN Instagram account, using
      your own phone or an authenticator app
  [ ] Turn on two-factor authentication on your personal Instagram account, same
      phone or app
  [ ] Confirm the recovery email and phone on both IG accounts are YOURS only
  [ ] Turn on two-factor authentication on the recovery email account itself
  [ ] Turn on login alerts on both IG accounts so you get pinged on any new login
  [ ] Decide your password will NOT be shared with him, ever


PHASE 2 - GRANT LIMITED ACCESS (no password)

  [ ] Make sure both IG accounts are Professional (Business or Creator) accounts
  [ ] Set up a Meta Business Portfolio (Business Manager) that you own
  [ ] Add both IG accounts as assets inside your Business Portfolio
  [ ] Confirm you are listed as Owner / Admin of the Business Portfolio
  [ ] Have your cousin create or use his OWN Meta account (his own login)
  [ ] Invite him as a person with PARTIAL access (not full control)
  [ ] Grant only the content / scheduling permission
  [ ] Turn OFF full control
  [ ] Turn OFF manage account / settings / billing
  [ ] Do NOT make him an admin of the Business Portfolio
  [ ] Confirm he can open the Planner and schedule, but cannot reach account
      settings or your password


PHASE 3 - OPTIONAL MAXIMUM CONTROL (publish gate)

  [ ] If you want a final sign-off before anything goes live, use a third-party
      scheduler with an approval workflow (Later or Metricool)
  [ ] Set him as a contributor who drafts/schedules only
  [ ] Set yourself as the approver so nothing publishes without your okay


PHASE 4 - WHILE HE WORKS (monitoring)

  [ ] Check Where You're Logged In on both accounts every few days
  [ ] Remote-log-out any device or session you do not recognize
  [ ] Watch for login alert emails and confirm they are him, not a stranger
  [ ] Spot-check the Planner against your posting schedule so nothing is wrong
      or missing


PHASE 5 - WHEN THE JOB IS DONE (revoke)

  [ ] Remove his person/access from the Business Portfolio
  [ ] If you used a third-party tool, remove him as a contributor there too
  [ ] Change your Instagram passwords as hygiene (both accounts)
  [ ] Confirm two-factor authentication is still on and still tied to your phone
  [ ] Re-check Where You're Logged In and log out anything unexpected


KEY RULE: never share your password, keep two-factor on your own phone, and give
only a scheduling-level role tied to his own login. With that, a takeover is not
possible and you can cut his access in one click.
