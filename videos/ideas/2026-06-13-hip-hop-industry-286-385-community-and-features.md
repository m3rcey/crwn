Generated 2026-06-13. Continuation of the shortform Hip-Hop Industry series (scripts 1-285 already written/queued). All `[S]` short-form. Numbered as the series running order (286-385).

**What's different about this batch:** scripts 1-285 lean almost entirely on the monetization angles (own the line, keep more, see the money). This batch rotates the *other* real CRWN capabilities to the front, with the community/connection angle as the lead. Each line ends with a bracketed `[capability]` tag so you can see which CRWN feature it sets up. The capability is a *suggestion*, not a sales pitch, and it isn't forced into every idea.

**Format note:** these are TOPICS + one-line synopses. Hand each to `/crwn-shortform` to write the script. Every hard figure/date gets web-verified at script-writing time (per the fact-check protocol); synopses use iconic verified facts or hedge ("reportedly/around"); don't ship a number unconfirmed.

**Grounded in the verified feature set (audited 2026-06-13).** Community is a shared feed where fans post, comment on each other (fan-to-fan), like, and earn tier badges. It does NOT have polls, voting/idea-boards, fan-to-fan DMs, or group chat, so no idea claims those. Other real capabilities used here: paid experiences + 1-on-1 Calendly booking, sync-opportunity matching, AI Manager (churn/VIP/insights), audience segmentation, email campaigns + automated sequences, referral/share-and-earn, smart links + pre-save capture + discount codes, supporter wall + community badges, digital/physical/experience products. NOT claimed (not built): livestreaming, NFTs, native DMs, merch fulfillment, SMS-to-fans UI.

## Q. Know your fans / AI manager / see the money (analytics, segmentation, AI) — 336-347

336. [S] Bad Bunny is the most-streamed artist alive and still can't email a single one of those listeners. The platforms show play counts, not people, and keep the contacts. Own the data and your audience becomes a list you can actually reach. [Analytics / own the data]
337. [S] Russ posts his own numbers because he actually reads them. Most artists guess at what's working because the platforms hide the math. See the real numbers and the guessing stops. [Analytics]
338. [S] Beyoncé's Hive and a casual playlist-adder are not the same fan. Your audience is really six groups, new, casual, loyal, at-risk, lapsed, gone, and each needs a different move. Segment them and you stop sending one message to six different people. [Audience segmentation]
339. [S] What Chris Lighty did managing 50 Cent, software can do for an artist with no budget. An AI manager spots your VIPs, suggests the move, makes it, then measures whether it worked. The job a label took a cut for, running on its own. [AI manager]
340. [S] RZA got film and scoring work because someone connected him to it; the match is the hard part. Software can point your genre at the shows and ads hunting for it. The opportunity exists; you just can't see the whole board. [AI manager / sync]
341. [S] An Erykah Badu fan who's stayed 20 years is worth more than a one-time buyer. A $10 subscriber who sticks beats a $40 one-off, every time. See the long number and you stop chasing the wrong sale. [Analytics]
342. [S] 50 Cent stays relevant by reading the moment and striking on time. Timing a win-back to the week a fan cools is everything. Knowing WHEN is worth more than the code itself. [AI manager / discount codes]
343. [S] A&Rs used to get paid to spot who's about to pop; a score does it for you. Some fans are warming up to buy and you can't feel it through a follower count. Engagement scoring points you at the next yes. [Audience / lead scoring]
344. [S] The fan who's one nudge from subscribing is the one a good manager would call. High-intent fans sit in your audience unconverted. Spot them and a small push closes the gap. [Lead scoring]
345. [S] Jay-Z reads his business like an exec; a dashboard gives any artist the same view. Which tier grows, which product stalls, which month dipped and why. The numbers are a manager that never lies to you. [Analytics]
346. [S] The money lands in your account, not a label's, and you watch it arrive. Every subscription and sale pays out to you directly, minus a small platform cut you can see. Nobody stands between you and the dollar. [Payouts / see the money]
347. [S] Drake can A/B a rollout with a whole team; the data lets you do it solo. Which song actually turns a listener into a buyer? Plays don't tell you, but the numbers behind them do. Find the track that converts and make more of it. [Analytics]
